High-Speed Precision Steering Mirror (FSM) PID Control System

Micro-Precision Optical Tracking via Dynamic System Modeling

MATLAB Simulink PID Tuning Frequency Response Analysis

🔹Context & Objective

Overview: 2-axis High-Speed Fine Steering Mirrors (FSM) are critical components in precision laser communication and optical tracking systems.

Objective: Design, simulate, and implement a robust feedback control loop to optimize target tracking speed while suppressing high-frequency mechanical resonances during a Control R&D Internship.

🔹Technical Implementation

1. Plant Modeling & Simulation

  • Modeled the electro-mechanical dynamics of the FSM system within `MATLAB` and `Simulink`.
  • Identified system transfer functions by analyzing frequency response data (Bode plots) to locate structural resonance points.

2. PID Controller Design

  • Developed an advanced PID control architecture with a low-pass filter on the derivative term (PID + D_Filter) to suppress sensor noise.
  • Tuned controller gains (Kp, Ki, Kd) to achieve a phase margin greater than 60°, ensuring robust stability against plant parameter variations.

3. Performance Optimization

  • Conducted loop-shaping to maximize tracking bandwidth while maintaining safe gain and phase margins.

🔹Deliverables & Troubleshooting

Key Challenge: High-frequency structural resonance caused the closed-loop system to become unstable and oscillate when the controller gains were increased for faster response times.

Solution: Designed and inserted a digital Notch Filter into the forward control path within Simulink, successfully attenuating the resonant peak without degrading low-frequency tracking bandwidth.
