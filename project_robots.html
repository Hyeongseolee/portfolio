Multi-Axis Autonomous Logistics Robot Control Architecture

Kinematic Modeling and Field-Proven Fleet Operations

C++ Robot Kinematics Multi-Motor Sync Embedded Firmware

🔹Context & Objective

Overview: Development of specialized autonomous mobile robots (AMR) optimized for warehouse pick-and-place and sorting logistics.

Achievements: 2nd Place (Patent Commissioner's Award) at the 2024 World Creative Robot Contest (WCRC), and 3rd Place at the Hong Kong RoboGames (Logistics Robot Division).

🔹Technical Implementation

1. Kinematics Modeling

  • Formulated exact Forward and Inverse Kinematics models in `C++` using transformation matrices to orchestrate precise omnidirectional mobility.
  • Calibrated odometry and wheel-velocity synchronization algorithms to minimize dead-reckoning errors during high-speed transit.

2. Embedded Control & Firmware

  • Programmed low-level microcontrollers to handle high-frequency encoder feedback and real-time current/velocity control loops for individual actuators.

🔹Deliverables & Troubleshooting

Hong Kong Field Incident: Unexpected arena lighting conditions blinded the optical alignment sensors, and a slick floor surface caused severe wheel slippage, throwing off the robot's coordinate positioning during the competition.

Solution: Led an emergency on-field code refactoring session. Implemented an adaptive thresholding algorithm for the optical sensors and integrated an acceleration-limiting profile in the C++ firmware to eliminate wheel slip under low-friction conditions, leading the team to a podium finish.
